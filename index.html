Academic Research & Portfolio

Highlighting my major academic projects from Kathmandu University focused on geospatial hazard modeling.

Multi-Hazard Risk Assessment of Dhading District

GIS AHP (Analytical Hierarchy) Hazard Modeling Remote Sensing

Overview: Conducted a comprehensive multi-hazard risk assessment of Dhading District by integrating physical hazard data with socio-economic vulnerability metrics.

  • Mapped susceptibility for floods, landslides, and forest fires using over 16 conditioning factors (Slope, TWI, NDVI, LST, etc.).
  • Developed a Socio-Economic Vulnerability Index (SEVI) utilizing National Census data across social, economic, and physical dimensions.
  • Utilized the Analytical Hierarchy Process (AHP) in ArcGIS to assign systematic weights to hazard variables, achieving highly consistent (CR < 0.1) matrices.
  • Validated spatial models using ROC-AUC curves, achieving excellent predictive accuracies (>0.85) for all hazard maps.
View Research Report

Soil Erosion Estimation of Dhading Using the RUSLE Model

RUSLE Model Spatial Analysis Environmental Monitoring ArcGIS

Overview: Developed an empirical model to quantify spatial soil loss patterns in Dhading District to support agricultural and conservation planning.

  • Integrated multiple data sources including ALOS PALSAR DEM, DHM rainfall data, FAO soil maps, and Landsat/Sentinel LULC data.
  • Calculated primary RUSLE factors: Rainfall erosivity (R), Soil erodibility (K), Topographic parameters (LS), Cover management (C), and Support practices (P).
  • Estimated a total annual mean soil loss of 10.4 million tonnes across the district.
  • Categorized the district into erosion severity classes, identifying critical zones (>80 t/ha/yr) requiring immediate conservation prioritization.
